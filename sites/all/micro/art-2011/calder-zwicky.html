Calder Zwicky is Associate Educator of Teen and Community Programs at the Museum of Modern Art in New York City, NY. In this capacity, he oversees MoMA’s Community Partnership Program; an initiative that creates art programs in collaboration with a cohort of 30 community-based organizations working with a wide-range of audiences including incarcerated and post-incarcerated youth, adult literacy students, drug treatment centers, job training programs, and people living with HIV/AIDS.

In addition to his work with community programs, Calder oversees the Museum’s long-term, art making and behind-the-scenes programming for NYC high school students. He has worked for a wide range of arts-based institutions including the Walker Art Center (1997-2002), The Studio Museum in Harlem (2003), Lehman College’s Fine Arts Gallery (2004-5), and the Bronx Museum of the Arts (2003-2010).
