Cheri Ehrlich is a Senior Museum Educator and the Teen Programs Coordinator at the Brooklyn Museum in Brooklyn, New York. She is currently working toward her Ed.D. in Art and Art Education at Teachers College Columbia University, where she also received her Ed.M. Ms. Ehrlich's research focuses on Aesthetic Education and Adolescent Development. She holds a M.A.T. in Art from Tufts University and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts, and both a B.F.A. in Painting and a B.A. in Womenâ€™s Studies from the University of Massachusetts Amherst.

Ms. Ehrlich enjoys working with high school students because they are full of energy and great ideas. Since 2006, Ms. Ehrlich has been overseeing the Museum Apprentice Program in which teens learn to teach younger audiences about artworks. In 2009 she started Teen Night Planning Committee, teaching teen interns how to coordinate, promote, and implement Teen Night events for the Museum's teen visitors.
