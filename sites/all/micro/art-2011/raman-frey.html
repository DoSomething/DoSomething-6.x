Raman Frey is co-owner (with Wendi Norris) of Frey Norris Contemporary & Modern, one of San Francisco's landmark galleries since 2002, with parallel programs in contemporary and modern art. Frey Norris occupies a 5,100 square foot Gensler designed space in the SoMA neighborhood of San Francisco. The gallery exhibits a varied and accomplished roster of contemporary artists from the world over, including Kate Eric, Sherin Guirguis, Tomokazu Matsuyama, Lionel Bawden, Zhong Biao and Julio Cesar Morales.

Frey has a background and historical expertise in artists associated with surrealism, such as Leonora Carrington, Remedios Varo, Dorothea Tanning and Wolfgang Paalen, all four of whom have appeared with major survey exhibitions at Frey Norris. The gallery has placed works with numerous museums, including SFMOMA, The Art Institute of Chicago, The de Young Museum and the FEMSA Collection in Monterrey, Mexico. He currently serves on the Executive Committee of the board at the Yerba Buena Center for the Arts, an inter-disciplinary arts non-profit with programs in visual art, film and performance.
